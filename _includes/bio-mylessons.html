My Lessons
You can find more meaningful patterns in data and come up with a valuable observation if you understand the broader context.
The best projects are likely to be overlooked, not trumpeted by a crowd; the best problems to work on are often the ones nobody else even tries to solve.
The secret of success lies in the organization of the non-obvious.
It's important to develop the ability to continuously acquire new and better forms of knowledge that we can apply to our work and to our lives.
